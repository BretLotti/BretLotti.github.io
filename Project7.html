SLA Priting

This is the first machine we did not get trained on the machine for the project. We uploaded our designs and had them printed for us. So my explanations are through second hand knowledge. One key differnce between SLA and FDM printing, is the material used. While FDM priting uses filament, which is a heated plastic. SLA printing uses a type of resin. SLA printing also requires more care as the liquid resin can cause rashes when exposed to skin. Also, while the build plate of an FDM printer is on the bottom and and object is created from the top. An SLA printer does the opposite and brings the build plate downwards and the resin is polymerized from the bottom. An object made through SLA printing is also somewhat soft after the print is finished and needs to be washed and then cured in order to completely finish a project.

New Skills

The first new skill I picked up was using the line tool in Fusion. As it was the basis of getting the ring design to work. For my other projects, I used the rectangle and circle tools, but never touched the line tool until now. Getting the hang of it took a little while and required some trial and error, but I eventually figured it out.

The next new tool I learned was "revolve". This is what actaully tunred the line work into the ring the shape. I honestly don't have much else to say on the revolve tool. It revolved a sketch around a selected axis. And that is pretty much what I know about it.

Next I learned to use was "Section Analysis" This allowed me to cut away a part of my design and look inside of it. This was very important as the spring is two parts that are interlocked, which is what allows it to spin. The section analysis tool allowed me to look and see if the two parts were created correctly.

To make my design more comfortable and ring shaped. I used the "fillet tool". This allowd me to curve the edges of the ring and the interlocking pieces to help it spin. That's really the main focus of the tool. Smooth edges and other parts of designs made in Fusion.

Finally, I learned to add text to my designs inside Fusion. When I made my desk organizer, I didn't use the text feature in Fusion at all. However, the ring required me to use the text tool in order to add text to my design. I kept my text rather simple and just made it my name. I am pretty happy with how it came out and I think it fits with the size of the ring really well.
