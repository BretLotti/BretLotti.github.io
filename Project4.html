Digital Embroidery


Project Details

This weeks project was to design a logo for a fake company in adobe Illustrator and then embrioder said design onto a draw string bag.

Logo Design Process

I knew almost instanly that I wanted to have my logo and fake company name be based around skateboarding. My first step here was to create a name for this skateboad company. I then thought of the word "wicked" which is slang most popularly used in New England states such as RI(Where I am from) and Mass. The word also fits nicely into what is generally considered to be "Skater Slang". So I had the name "Wicked Skateboards". The rest of the design came pretty easily. I used a font that had a Halloween/Skater look to it for the company name. Orignally, the design was to use black thread for the text and character, but the black thread refused to work. So I had to use a different color, which ended up being red. I then used orange for the framing device since the idea was for it to be a pumpkin.



The Embroidery/Machine Struggles

This has by far been the most frustrating project for this class thus far. The machine was broken for a few days. So I could not schedule a time to get in the lab to make the bag with my design. And when I managed to get to the lab after the machine had been fixed. I was running into issues where the machine kept saying that it was not threaded properly, despite the fact I was certain I was threading it correctly. And there was no one in the lab was experienced enough on the machine to help me trouble shoot the issue. So after two hours of trying. I had to throw in the towel and come back another day. After that, I came back a few days later and learned the black thread I was trying to use was just not working with the machine and I had to use another color in my design. So, after a week of issues, I was finally able to get my design embroidered onto the fabric. However, I ran out of my booked time and needed to pack up for a training to take place. I then came in a few days later and was able to stitch my bag together. Finally completing the project(Thankfully).
