FDM Printing


Project Details

This weeks project was to design and print a pot using using an FDM printer that can fit into the desk organizer from the last project.

FDM Printers

FDM printers can be thought of as almost high tech glue guns. They take a design from and STL file and super height a filiment that the design will be made of. After the heating process is done, it begins to create the physical object. This printer goes layer,by layer, until the object is complete. This is a slow process that can take several hours. My pot alone took almost 10 hours to complete.

Prusa Slicer

Prusa Slicer is one of the programs I used during the creation of my pot. It can be seen as almsot a simulation of the FDM printer. It will show you an exact cpoy of the the bed of the FDM printer as well as what your design will look like as it starts to print. It also provides helpful information, such as the amount of time the print will take, the size of object on the bed, and if you need any supports. The FDM printer cannot create in thin air, so parts of an object that are hanging off, or not on the bed, need supports in order to print.

Fusion 360

This whole Project started in Fusion 360. I started by creating the base of my pot that would fit into the desk organzier. This involved setting the dimensions correctly to make sure it would fit after being created. I then created a rounded body with a squared off top. I messed around with a couple design ideas, but ended up liking the rounded body with a sqaure top the most. I then added some "wings", for lack of a better word, to make my design stand out a bit more. The final step was hollowing it out, since it was supposed to be a pot after all. After I was happy with the design it was simply a matter of exporting it as an STL.
